SIR (Susceptible, Infected, Recovered) epidemic model.

Parameters
[in]netThe network the epidemic model is applied on
[in]p0Initial proportion of infected individuals. Used as a probability of being infected at the beginning of the simulation, applied to each individual.
[in]betaRate of infection of an individual with a single neighbour
[in]gammaRate of recovery of an individual
[in]TMaximum number of steps of the simulation. The steps performed have index 1,2,...,T
[in]drgThe discrete random generator used for algorithm optimisation
[in]crgThe continuous random generator used to generate numbers between 0 and 1
[out]n_recn_rec[i] contains the amount of recoverd agents after the i-th step is completed. n_rec[0] always has a zero and is not a valid value. Its size at the end of the simulation T + 1.
[out]n_susn_sus[i] contains the amount of susceptible agents after the i-th step is completed. n_sus[0] contains the amount of agents susceptible of infection after infecting a proportion of p0 agents of the population. Its size at the end of the simulation is T + 1.
[out]n_infn_inf[i] contains the amount of infected agents after the i-th step is completed. n_inf[0] contains the amount of agents susceptible of infection after infecting a proportion of p0 agents of the population. Its size at the end of the simulation T + 1.
